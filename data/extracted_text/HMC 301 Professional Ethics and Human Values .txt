Q3 
Read the case carefully  
Shreya is a senior accountant at a mid-sized manufacturing company. 
She has been with the company for five years and is highly regarded for 
her meticulous work and dedication. Recently, she noticed some 
discrepancies in the financial statements that could indicate potential 
fraud. The irregularities involve overstating revenue in order to meet 
quarterly targets, which would make the company’s performance look 
better to investors.  
She faces a difficult choice. On one hand, she feels obligated to report 
these irregularities to higher management or external auditors, as 
ignoring them could lead to severe consequences for the company, 
employees, and shareholders. On the other hand, she fears retaliation, 
job loss, and damage to her career if she brings the issue to light, 
especially since the company’s CFO seems to be involved in the 
fraudulent activity. 
Answer the following- 
1. What should Shreya do in this situation? Should she report the 
irregularities, and if so, to whom?  
2. How can She protect herself if she decides to report the fraud?  
3. What role does the company’s culture play in how She perceives 
her options?  
4. How might this situation have been prevented with better 
corporate governance and internal controls?
